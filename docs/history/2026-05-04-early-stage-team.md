# Early Stage Team — Who to Find

**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Status:** To review

---

## Who You Need at the Early Stage

### 1. A Co-founder, Not a Hireling

The most important person is someone who shares the problem personally — a Vietnamese professional who is actively preparing for IELTS or recently went through it under real time pressure. They do not have to be a software engineer, but they must understand deeply why generic IELTS content fails working professionals. They need skin in the game, not a salary.

**What they bring:** User empathy from their own domain (engineering, healthcare, finance, education), credibility with the target audience, time you don't have, and the willingness to do unglamorous work (seeding groups, talking to beta users, writing content).

**Where to find them:** Your existing network first. Former colleagues at tech companies or other professional fields. Post in Facebook groups like "Vietnam Software Engineer Community", "Cộng đồng IELTS Việt Nam", or professional groups for nurses/finance workers — someone there is frustrated with generic tools and has the energy to help build the right alternative.

---

### 2. An IELTS Expert (Part-time / Advisory)

The AI scoring is the core value proposition. You need one certified IELTS examiner or experienced prep teacher to:
- Validate the AI scoring output (critical for trust)
- Write or review the prompt library and example essays
- Act as a public face or endorser

**Compensation:** Free lifetime access + revenue share or a small monthly retainer. Many teachers in Vietnam run small private classes and would value the association with a tech product.

---

### 3. A Content Creator (Not a Developer)

The bottleneck at early stage is not the product — it is getting the first 100 users. You need someone who can produce Vietnamese content consistently: short videos, Facebook posts, Zalo community management.

This is a CS student or junior developer who is also studying IELTS and active on social media. They work for equity or a small stipend in exchange for building their own audience alongside the product.

---

## What You Should NOT Do Early

- Do not hire a freelance developer to add features. The codebase is solid. Features are not the constraint.
- Do not pay an agency for marketing. You need authentic community presence, not ads.
- Do not bring in a business partner who has never personally experienced the mismatch between their professional knowledge and generic IELTS content — they will not understand why the positioning matters.

---

## Practical First Step

Write a one-paragraph post in Vietnamese and share it in 2–3 Facebook groups:

> "Mình đang xây dựng một tool luyện IELTS cho người đi làm có deadline thi — chấm bài Writing và Speaking bằng AI theo tiêu chí thật của IELTS, với nội dung xoay quanh lĩnh vực chuyên môn của người học thay vì những chủ đề chung chung. Hiện tại đang tập trung vào nhóm kỹ sư phần mềm. Mình đang tìm người đồng hành ở giai đoạn đầu: có thể là một kỹ sư hoặc chuyên gia đang ôn IELTS, một giáo viên IELTS muốn thử AI, hoặc ai đó thích làm content. Nếu bạn tò mò, nhắn tin cho mình."

That one post will tell you within 48 hours whether there is an audience and who wants to help.
